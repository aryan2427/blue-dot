Blue Dot Economy DPGs
Configurable Digital Public Infrastructure for Local Discovery

The Problem: Local Discovery Failure

Many of India's progressive districts are not suffering from a resource failure—shortage of jobs, skills, or services. They are suffering from a local discovery failure: the livelihoods, talent, schemes, and services exist, in proximity, but cannot discover each other.

The Paradox of Proximity

Local supply and demand sit kilometers apart—sometimes meters apart—and function as though they belong to entirely separate economies.

Examples:

* An ITI graduate goes unplaced while a nearby SMB needs that exact trade.
* A qualified woman wants to return to work while a nearby business needs an accountant.
* A person with locomotor disability needs assistive devices while an NGO nearby helps people apply for schemes.
* A tourist misses authentic experiences while local tour operators remain undiscovered.
* A business needs food services while nearby catering units seek customers.
* A student travels long distances for coaching while a qualified teacher lives nearby.

Close in distance. Invisible in discovery.

Why This Happens

District economies are digitally dark. Most livelihoods, services, schemes, and opportunities are not digitally mapped. Information travels through posters, word-of-mouth, walls, and informal networks.

National platforms are not designed for the vernacular, fragmented, hyperlocal realities of district economies.

The Cost

* SMBs contribute nearly 70% of employment but only around 30% of GDP.
* Placement rates remain below 50%.
* Scheme utilization remains below 70%.
* Discovery failure prevents nearby demand and supply from connecting.

Discovery Failure Impacts Vulnerable Populations Most

Those most affected include:

* Women re-entering the workforce
* Persons with disabilities
* First-generation graduates
* Daily wage workers

For them, discovery failure becomes the primary barrier to economic participation.

The Proof: Ghaziabad and Dharwad Pilots

Key Outcomes:

* Nearly 10,000 local job openings surfaced.
* Voice AI reduced discovery costs from ₹500 to ₹10.
* Discovery time reduced from over two weeks to less than one day.
* Placement rates improved from below 10% to above 50%.
* Local employment reduced long-distance migration.

The pilots did not create new demand and supply. They revealed what already existed locally.

The Solution: Three Configurable DPGs

Blue Dot Economy DPGs are configurable open-source infrastructure components enabling local discovery and coordination.

The Three DPGs:

1. Signal DPG

* Enables individuals to register, create profiles, discover opportunities, and track interactions.
* Users include job seekers, employers, service providers, beneficiaries, tourists, and homestay owners.

Core capabilities:

* Custom profile creation
* Searchable registry
* Connection tracking
* Credential verification
* OTP authentication
* Geographic map discovery

2. Aggregator DPG

* Enables intermediary organizations to onboard participants at scale and track outcomes.
* Used by NGOs, training institutes, tourism offices, and placement agencies.

Core capabilities:

* Bulk onboarding
* Participant tracking
* Profile completion monitoring
* Reporting dashboards

3. Ecosystem Facilitator DPG

* Provides ecosystem-wide visibility and coordination.
* Used by district officials, state administrators, and mission directorates.

Core capabilities:

* Cross-network dashboards
* Demand-supply analysis
* Aggregator performance monitoring
* Intervention coordination

Shared Digital Infrastructure

Blue Dot Economy DPGs function similarly to UPI for discovery and coordination.

They provide shared digital rails enabling:

* Discoverability
* Matching
* Verification
* Coordination
* Interoperability

Schema-Driven Configuration

Departments define their own schemas, workflows, participant types, and rules while using shared infrastructure.

Employment schema examples:

* Job seekers
* Employers
* Training providers

Tourism schema examples:

* Homestay owners
* Tourists
* Service providers

Welfare schema examples:

* Citizens
* Scheme providers
* Field officers

Interoperability Through Network Contracts

DPG instances can discover and interact across districts and departments using shared network contracts.

Examples:

* Training institutes can connect job seekers with employers.
* Aggregators across districts can coordinate.
* State authorities can monitor district ecosystems.

Joining an Existing Network

Organizations deploy only the DPGs relevant to their role.

Examples:

* Service providers deploy Signal DPG.
* NGOs deploy Signal + Aggregator DPG.
* State ecosystem creators deploy all three DPGs.

What This Means

Blue Dot Economy DPGs create shared digital discovery infrastructure enabling districts to discover and connect what already exists locally.

The infrastructure supports:

* Employment
* Tourism
* Welfare
* Education
* Agriculture
* Service ecosystems

The system enables structured visibility, verified connections, interoperability, and ecosystem coordination.

Key Principle:
Deploy only what you need.
